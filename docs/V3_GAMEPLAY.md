# V3 Gameplay Specification

## Core skill

The player reads ball ownership and speed, positions, times a defensive action,
then returns the ball with intentional direction.

Skill dimensions:

- Timing.
- Aim.
- Movement.
- Shot selection.
- Risk management.
- Team positioning.

## Rally Duel

- 1v1.
- No runes, passives, active skills, ultimates or random power-ups.
- Same HP, speed, stamina and deflect power.
- Normal, great and perfect deflect tiers.
- First stable ruleset uses existing round scoring.
- Empty online slots may use a clearly labelled bot.

## Team Arena

- 3v3 casual first.
- Team pass/assist credit.
- Bot backfill.
- Short reconnect window.
- Character abilities remain a separate casual identity layer.

## Catch/parry prototype

- Separate input from normal deflect.
- Smaller timing window.
- Failure creates recovery vulnerability.
- Success grants controlled possession, not guaranteed damage.
- Must not become the universally best defensive action.

## Ball readability

- Team ownership color.
- Speed-driven heat state.
- Directional threat indicator.
- Layered audio by distance and speed.
- Reduced particle density near crosshair.
- No full-screen flash hiding the trajectory.

## Arcade

One rotating slot:

- Speedball.
- Multiball.
- Hot Potato.
- Pinball.
- Low Gravity.
- Instagib.

Only one is promoted at a time to avoid queue fragmentation.

## Balance data

- Deflect success by tier.
- Rally duration.
- Round duration.
- Shot selection.
- Catch success/failure.
- Map win side.
- Character pick/win rate in ability-enabled casual.
- Quit after deaths or ability events.
